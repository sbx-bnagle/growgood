I&rsquo;m curious about hardscape materials that are locally sourced. One thing we don&rsquo;t see around here too often is a wood paved path. But, this used to be a big thing. The neighborhood we currently live in sprung up around a private wood plank road, one of several that spoked out from the city often referred to as the &lsquo;farmer&rsquo;s railroad&rsquo;. [Eventually the roads were annexed into the city, the tollbooths burnt down, and the their owner murdered.](https://chicagoandcookcountycemeteries.com/2024/08/05/chicagos-plank-roads-or-being-stuck-in-the-mud/) ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯.

## Nicholson pavement

It wasn&rsquo;t just us hayseeds promenading on the planks. Urban roads were paved with wood pavers as well. The [Nicolson Pavement Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolson_pavement) of Boston enjoyed popularity all over the States while stone was scarce and wood pavers were seen as quieter alternative with all the horses stomping around. A couple alleys around here still have them, and they&rsquo;re exposed occasionally at the bottom of some seismic-level potholes.
